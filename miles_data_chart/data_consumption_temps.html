Can Global Warming Impact Your Utility Bill?

A Look at 2017 Weather and Energy Consumption Patterns

By: Avery Miles

  • Story
  • Energy Temps
  • About
As “hotumn” roared through the country this past fall, New Yorkers took shelter inside their cooled interiors. Climate change scientists have cited the costs of rising temperatures on the planet, but how is the changing weather also affecting how New Yorkers—both tenants and landlords alike—keep themselves cool?

Warmer temperatures lead to melting snowcaps and collapsed Antarctic ice shelves but they can also cause your monthly electricity bill to increase. The average New Yorker might not think or care about the rising ocean waters but they do care about staying comfortable.

"There is nothing basic about the electricity market!"

In a report conducted by New York Independent System Operator, energy consumption was tracked based on aggregate data from multiple energy supplies from around the state. "There is nothing basic about the electricity market," said a representative from the NYISO.

Here we take a look at this past year's weather patterns compared to the rates of energy consumption throughout New York State. This is the beginning of a longer report.
