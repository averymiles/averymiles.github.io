There's Something Fishy Going On

What do New Yorkers Think About Their Water?

The water is the cleanest in the world and the bagels taste delicious. In New York City, residents do not need Brita filters because the water is just superb.

According to the NYC Department of Environmental Protection, “New York City drinking water is world-renowned for its quality.” Water flows from upstate reservoirs in Putnam and Westchester counties and the Catskills to serve nine million customers throughout New York state, amounting to roughly 1 billion gallons of fresh water each day.

Then why are New Yorkers complaining about it? Based on a collection of 311 complaints from this past year, concerned residents have been telling the city that they are not happy with their water. Most particularly, Brooklynites.

We were unable to reach an expert to comment on why Queens residents are not complaining as much as those in Brooklyn.

The overall problem seems not to lie with industrial contamination that usually afflicts underground water sources but from the dilapidated pipes.

“Lead is the most common reason that you run into a water problem in New York,” says Dr. Wilkenfeld, director of the Division of Occupational and Environmental Medicine at Winthrop University Hospital in Mineola.

For now, it seems like New Yorkers will have to check their pipes before making their morning tea.
